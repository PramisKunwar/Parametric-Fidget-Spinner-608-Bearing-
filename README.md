# Parametric Fidget Spinner (608 Bearing)
A sleek, balanced, and highly optimized parametric fidget spinner designed for everyday carry (EDC) and smooth spinning. This project was engineered from the ground up using precise 3D parametric modeling techniques to ensure perfect rotational symmetry and optimal weight distribution.

This model is a fantastic 3D printing project to test your printer's dimensional accuracy for snap-fit assemblies.

## Print Settings & Slicer Recommendations

To guarantee that the **Spinner Hubs** snap-fit perfectly into the **608 Bearing** and the bearing firmly seats inside the **Main Body**, use the following recommended print configurations:

* **Material:** PLA (Recommended for maximum rigidity), PETG, or ABS.
* **Infill:** **25% to 40%** * *Tip: Higher infill increases the weight of the outer arms, providing a greater moment of inertia and longer spin times!*
* **Infill Pattern:** Gyroid or Cubic (for uniform weight distribution).
* **Layer Height:** 0.2mm for the main body; 0.15mm for smoother hub caps.
* **Walls / Perimeters:** 3 to 4 perimeters (adds strength around the bearing pocket).
* **Supports:** **No supports required**. All parts are designed to print completely flat on the build plate.

---

##  Assembly Instructions

1. **Prep the Bearing:** If using a shielded steel 608 bearing, pry off the grease shields and flush out the factory grease using Isopropyl Alcohol (IPA). Relubricate with a single drop of light machine oil (or leave completely dry) for maximum, friction-free spin times.
2. **Press-Fit the Center:** Align your cleaned 608 bearing over the center pocket of the **Spinner Main Body** and press it completely flush.
3. **Install the Caps:** Push the male pin of one **Spinner Hub** through the center hole (8mm) of the bearing and snap the female hub cap securely onto it from the opposite side.
4. **Flick & Spin!** Your fidget spinner is now ready.
